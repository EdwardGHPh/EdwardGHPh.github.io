Research Interests

My work sits at the intersection of quantum physics and machine learning. I am broadly interested in condensed matter physics — particularly disordered systems and spin glasses — where frustration and complex energy landscapes give rise to rich emergent phenomena. A central tool in my work is Neural Quantum States (NQS), which I apply to ground-state problems in closed quantum systems and to the simulation of open quantum systems, including Markovian and non-Markovian dynamics within the POVM formulation of time evolution.

A parallel line of work focuses on Quadratic Unconstrained Binary Optimization (QUBO) problems: many hard combinatorial problems map to spin-glass Hamiltonians, and I investigate solving them using NQS methods and neutral-atom hardware (Rydberg atom QPUs), with particular emphasis on signed-interaction problems and how to implement them on hardware without resorting to gadgetization. I am also drawn to questions about what machine learning can teach us about physics itself — from the scaling laws of large models to symmetries and group actions within high-dimensional parameter spaces.
